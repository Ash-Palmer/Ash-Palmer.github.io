Salt Lake City Temple

"It is the largest LDS temple at 253,015 square feet. The location site was dedicated in 1853, it was the sixth temple completed by the church. The walls are quartz monzonite from Little Cottonwood Canyon, twenty miles away from the temple site. Oxen transported the quarried rock initially, but was later carried by rail at a much faster pace. The temple took forty years to build, being dedicated in 1893."
